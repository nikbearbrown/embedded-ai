## Figure 5: Annotated power profile of one duty cycle. The radio is the biggest single feature — finding this on a real device usually finds the binding constraint.* The pattern looks like: baseline sleep current (5–50 µA), a sensor-acquisition spike (10–50 ms at 5–20 mA), an inference spike (50–500 ms at 30–200 mA), and back to baseline. Energy per inference is the integral of $V \times I$ over the active interval; for a 3.3 V device with constant 80 mA and 150 ms active, energy is $3.3 \cdot 0.08 \cdot 0.15 = 0.0396\ \text{J} = 39.6\ \text{mJ}$. If inference happens every 30 seconds, average power is 1.32 mW, and a 2,000 mAh / 6,600 mWh / 23,760 J battery lasts $23{,}760 / 0.00132 \approx 18\ \text{million seconds}$, or 208 days. Add sleep current at 10 µA and the sleep contribution is 0.985 mJ per cycle on top of the 39.6 mJ of inference — a 2.4% adjustment, which is small here because duty cycle is low, but which dominates as duty cycle rises.
